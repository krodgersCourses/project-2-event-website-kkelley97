The Event Story

From Field to Feed

Silage is livestock feed. It is the process of preserving grains and stocks with moisture in them for the livestock animal to eat throug the winter. In the summer crops consists of tritacale, a three way mix, or hay. Other plants can also be silaged like turnip tops. The object of preserving silage is the removal of oxygen. The equiptment needed to complete the Hamar job is a swather, chopper, trucks, and pack tractor.

In the Chopper Seat

Every July we go to Hamar to chop siliage. On this farm/ranch the silage will feed sheep and cows. Our fearless leader and chopper driver is Dave. His partner is Steve. Don't forget Dave's wife, Michelle. Dave hires a crew when its time to chop, and has found along with men, women are great drivers and workers.

The Process

There is a process to make silage. First, because we are making a grain type silage, the crop needs to be swathed. Next, it dries until it reaches the moisture level of 68%. Then the chopper picks up the windrows up, and runs it through the chopper. It is chopped into little pieces and spit into a silage truck. The truck takes it to the pit where it is unloaded. The pack tractor is next; it piles and packs the silage to remove the oxygen. The better the pack, the better the silage. After the job is done the pile is covered to keep oxygen and weather out.
